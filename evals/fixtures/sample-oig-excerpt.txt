MEMORANDUM FOR THE ADMINISTRATOR

FROM: Office of Inspector General
SUBJECT: Review of Procurement Controls in the Regional Office, FY2024

This memorandum transmits the results of our review of procurement controls at the Regional Office covering fiscal year 2024.

Background

The Regional Office processed approximately 1,400 procurement transactions during the review period, totaling $42.8 million. We selected a stratified sample of 90 transactions for testing, including all transactions over $250,000 and a random sample below that threshold.

Findings

Our testing identified three significant deficiencies in the procurement process.

First, in 18 of 90 sampled transactions (20%), required market research documentation was either missing or inadequate to support the contracting officer's price determination. This represents a material weakness in the office's compliance with FAR Part 10.

Second, the office's contract files for sole-source awards over $1 million did not consistently include the justification and approval documentation required by FAR 6.303. Of 12 sole-source awards reviewed, four lacked complete J&A documentation, and an additional two contained J&A documents that did not address all required elements.

Third, segregation of duties controls were not consistently enforced. In 7 transactions, the same individual served as both the requisitioner and the contracting officer's technical representative, creating a control gap that elevates the risk of undetected error or fraud.

Recommendations

We recommend that the Administrator take the following actions:

Recommendation 1: Issue updated guidance to all contracting officers reinforcing the FAR Part 10 market research documentation requirements, with specific examples of acceptable documentation.

Recommendation 2: Establish a quarterly file review process for all sole-source awards over $1 million to verify J&A completeness before contract award.

Recommendation 3: Update the office's procurement standard operating procedure to formally prohibit the dual role of requisitioner and COTR on the same transaction. The Administrator may also wish to consider implementing a system-level control in the procurement system that flags such conflicts.

Management Response

The Administrator concurred with all three recommendations. The office has committed to issuing updated market research guidance by March 31, 2025, and has already initiated procurement of a contract management system upgrade that will support the dual-role control flagging described in Recommendation 3.

We will follow up on implementation status in our FY2025 review.
